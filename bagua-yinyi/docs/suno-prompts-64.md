# 八音演易 · 六十四卦完整 Suno Prompt（无歌词纯器乐版）

## 通用约束

- Instrumental only, no vocals, no singing, no chanting
- Pure traditional Chinese instruments
- Ambient nature sounds integration
- No modern instruments, no electronic elements

## 第1章：乾下 ☰ — 编钟 + 天雷

### T01 乾为天 (#1)
Style: Ancient Chinese ceremonial, bianzhong bells, deep thunder, instrumental, epic, cosmic
Opening piece. A single bianzhong bell rings in vast silence. More bells join in a slow, majestic procession. Deep thunder rolls beneath like the heartbeat of heaven. The bells play in ascending intervals, creating a sense of cosmic unfolding. The piece builds to a grand peak, then subsides into distant thunder fading over the horizon. No vocals.

### T02 天泽履 (#10)
Style: Ancient Chinese, bianzhong bells, dizi flute, lake water, instrumental, cautious, graceful
Bianzhong bells establish a solemn foundation. A dizi flute enters with a careful, stepping melody — like walking on thin ice. Gentle lake water sounds lap beneath. The flute steps around the bells, never quite landing. The mood is alert, graceful, measured. The piece ends with the flute fading into mist over the lake. No vocals.

### T03 天火同人 (#13)
Style: Ancient Chinese, bianzhong bells, pipa, fire crackling, instrumental, gathering, warm
Warm bianzhong bells ring in harmony. A pipa enters with bright, clustered notes like sparks rising to join the bells. Fire crackling provides a warm texture. The melody is inclusive, open — like people gathering in a torchlit hall. Builds to a glowing communal climax, then settles into embers and distant bell tolls. No vocals.

### T04 天雷无妄 (#25)
Style: Ancient Chinese, bianzhong bells, drum, thunder, instrumental, warning, powerful
Bianzhong bells ring in short, insistent phrases. A deep drum pounds at irregular intervals like warning heartbeats. Thunder rolls continuously beneath. The bells become more urgent, the drum more insistent. The piece simulates a gathering storm that never strikes — the tension of expectation without action. Ends abruptly. No vocals.

### T05 天风姤 (#44)
Style: Ancient Chinese, bianzhong bells, xiao flute, wind, instrumental, fleeting, chance encounter
Distant bianzhong bells ring softly. Wind blows through the bell tones, bending them. A xiao flute enters — its breathy tone carried by the same wind. The bells and xiao circle each other, meeting briefly, then parting. The piece is about chance encounters in a vast world. Brief, poignant. Ends with wind and a single bell. No vocals.

### T06 天水讼 (#6)
Style: Ancient Chinese, bianzhong bells, guzheng, water current, instrumental, conflict, tension
Bianzhong bells strike in opposition to flowing guzheng arpeggios. The bells represent authority — rigid, insistent. The guzheng represents the flow of events — fluid, evasive. They argue. Water current rushes beneath the conflict. The piece never resolves, ending mid-phrase with both instruments locked in disagreement. No vocals.

### T07 天山遁 (#33)
Style: Ancient Chinese, bianzhong bells, stone chime, mountain echo, instrumental, retreat, solitude
Bianzhong bells play a descending pattern — retreating from the heights. Stone chimes answer from the mountains. Mountain echoes repeat every note into silence. The melody becomes sparser, like a figure walking deeper into the hills. The last bell fades and only the mountain echo remains. No vocals.

### T08 天地否 (#12)
Style: Ancient Chinese, bianzhong bells, xun ocarina, earth resonance, instrumental, blocked, isolated
Bianzhong bells in the high register — isolated, unable to descend. The xun answers from below with low, earthy tones. They are separated by a register gap that never closes. Earth resonance rumbles beneath. The piece communicates separation and blockage. Ends with the bells fading above and the xun sustaining below, never meeting. No vocals.
